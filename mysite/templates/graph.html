Concept


  Here, we are using word embeddings to create perceptual maps of categories of certain brands or people. By using Principal Component Analysis (PCA), we can reduce the 300-dimension or 200-dimension word vectors down into a 2 dimensional space that is more easily interpreted (plotted above using the first and second principal components). Clearly, however, since we are losing hundreds of dimensions of information, these graphs contain only a fraction of the variance of the original hundred-dimensional space. Above, we list the variance captured by the first principal component, the second principal component, and the summation of the two.

  Moreover, as the label for the axes (which are the principal components) of the perceptual maps are unclear, we find a list of the adjectives that are closest to the principal components in the original word vector space in order to get a better idea of what the labels might be. These are listed to the right.
